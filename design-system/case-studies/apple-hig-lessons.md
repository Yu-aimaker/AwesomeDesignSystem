# Case Study Lessons — Apple Human Interface Guidelines

## What Apple publishes at depth

- **Design principles** across platforms
- **Foundations**: accessibility, app icons, color, layout, materials (incl. Liquid Glass era), typography
- **Platform-specific** designing for iOS/macOS/visionOS/watchOS/tvOS
- **Components** and **technologies** (Siri, widgets, etc.)
- Continuous **what's new** channel

## Transferable principles for AwesomeDS

1. **Platform literacy first** — respect system conventions before brand divergence.
2. **Materials & depth** are semantic (hierarchy), not pure decoration.
3. **Typography hierarchy** is a first-class foundation equal to color.
4. **Accessibility is foundational**, not a bolt-on chapter.
5. **Icons** have strict production systems (grid, weight, perspective).
6. **Spatial / multi-platform** thinking (safe areas, focus, input modality).

## Anti-imitation

- Do not copy SF Symbols, San Francisco, or Apple product chrome.
- Transfer *systems thinking* and foundation taxonomy.
