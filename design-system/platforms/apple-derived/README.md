# Apple-derived product doctrine

This module extracts transferable product discipline from Apple's current,
first-party design guidance. It is not an Apple look-alike kit. Do not copy
Apple chrome, redistribute Apple fonts, symbols, templates, device bezels, or
present Apple trade dress as an AwesomeDS asset.

Use this material when a product spans devices, inputs, constrained system
surfaces, permissions, spatial contexts, or agent-assisted design work.

## Modules

- [Product principles](./product-principles.md) — eight decision gates for
  deciding what deserves to exist and how it earns attention and trust.
- [Capability adaptation](./capability-adaptation.md) — adapt intent to the
  capabilities present instead of stretching one screen across platforms.
- [Glanceable and live surfaces](./glanceable-live-surfaces.md) — lifecycle
  contracts for widgets, live status, notifications, and ambient surfaces.
- [System-mediated actions](./system-mediated-actions.md) — semantic entities
  and actions that can safely appear outside the primary app.
- [Spatial interfaces](./spatial-interfaces.md) — comfort, depth, consent, and
  fallback rules for spatial presentation.
- [Trust, permissions, and icon systems](./trust-and-icons.md) — contextual
  permission requests and coherent, accessible symbol families.
- [Agent workflow](./agent-workflow.md) — go wide, remix, repeat with realistic
  content, edge cases, risk review, and human judgment.

## Coverage gate

A feature has not completed platform review until it declares presentation,
input, attention, navigation, accessibility, environment, fallback, privacy,
and restoration behavior. Screenshots and a single ideal-state demo are not
coverage.
