# Asset governance

## Principle

A brand kit is a versioned product surface. Guidance without usable, licensed,
traceable artifacts is incomplete; a download folder without governance is a
liability.

## Asset manifest

Every asset declares stable ID, title, purpose, kind, source master, derivative
relationship, format, dimensions/view box, color space, locale, version,
checksum, creator, owner, approver, copyright/license, usage restrictions,
territory, consent/release link, created/review/expiry dates, status, replacement,
export preset, accessibility metadata, and official/community designation.

## Pipeline

Masters are immutable per version. Reproducible export presets generate SVG,
PNG, PDF, or other approved derivatives; optimization cannot strip accessibility
or rights metadata. Publish checksums and a changelog. Downloads expose license,
version, file size, intended use, and support contact before transfer.

## Lifecycle

Use draft → approved → deprecated → withdrawn. Deprecation announces dates and
replacement; withdrawal removes download access without erasing audit history.
Expired rights automatically block publication. Community assets remain clearly
separated and never inherit official approval by proximity.

## QA evidence

Malware scan; checksum verification; SVG safety; format/open test; color and
transparency previews; minimum-size render; embedded-font/license check; rights
and expiry gate; keyboard-accessible downloads; broken-link test; restore drill.

## Support

Every collection has owner, response target, request form, misuse report route,
exception workflow, and escalation. Requests capture intended audience, medium,
territory, deadline, modifications, and rights—not merely “send logo.”
