# Goodpatch — Design Philosophy & Process

> **What this is:** An AI-agent-first knowledge file on Goodpatch (グッドパッチ), the Tokyo-based design firm that was the first design-led company to IPO in Japan. Covers mission/values, design process (the "Goodpatch DNA" and the Good Problems → Good People → Good Prototypes → Good Products chain), the Minimum Lovable Product (MLP) methodology, emotional/craft philosophy, Japanese design sensibility, products (Prott, ReDesigner, Strap, Goodpatch Anywhere), and transferable principles.
>
> **Status (June 2026):** Goodpatch Inc. (TSE: 7351) operates primarily from its Tokyo HQ. The European entity (Goodpatch GmbH, Berlin/Munich) has wound down; the company continues "to prove the power of design" mainly through Japan. Treat Berlin-era writing as historically authoritative for *methodology* even though the studios have closed.

## 2. The Mission: "Prove the Power of Design"

Goodpatch's entire identity is a thesis to be proven, not a slogan: design has *inherent power* to advance the world and solve business challenges. Two corollaries shape everything:

1. **Design is upstream, not decoration.** Goodpatch enters from the *strategy phase* — "they are not pure implementers." They interweave design, technology, and business from the start so design integrates with development rather than being bolted on.
2. **Design has measurable business value.** The 2020 IPO was framed as institutional proof that design is a business discipline, not a cost center.

> *"Goodpatch believes design possesses inherent power to progress the world and solve business challenges."* — goodpatch.com (mission page)

The vision word that recurs everywhere is **emotion / 心 / heart** — products that "move people emotionally" (人の心を動かす). This is the bridge from business-mission to the craft philosophy below.

## 3. The Goodpatch DNA — Their Design Process

Over years of building digital products, Goodpatch codified a **shared, reusable process** they call the **"Goodpatch DNA."** It is a double-diamond-style structure with two interlocking tracks:

```
                  ┌─────────────── DESIGN TRACK ───────────────┐
   PROBLEM   →    diverge (explore broadly)  →  converge (decide) →  PRODUCT
                  └──────────────────┬──────────────────────────┘
                                     │  continuous iteration if needed
                  ┌──────────────────┴──────────────────────────┐
                  │             FEEDBACK TRACK                    │
                  │   diverge (gather signals) → converge (learn) │
                  └───────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Design Track** and **Feedback Track** both pass through **divergent** (go broad, create opportunities) and **convergent** (narrow down, make informed decisions) phases.
- The two tracks run in parallel so the team can **iterate continuously**.
- Heart of the process (their words): *"allowing ourselves to go [broad] and create opportunities and subsequently narrowing our ideas down by making informed decisions."*

### 3.1 The four "Goods" — the macro flow

The DNA is taught publicly via Goodpatch's **"Prototyping As Mindset"** deck as a chain of four stages:

| Stage | Name | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Good Problems** | *Client Brief & Creative Scope.* Understand the challenge for client **and** user; judge whether client + Goodpatch are the right fit as a partnership. *"We are most excited to work on challenging problems to create meaningful products."* Start by identifying **Problems Worth Solving.** |
| 2 | **Good People** | *Team building.* Belief: **great teams build great products.** Reject the classic agency↔client split — build **one team** (shared space, shared infra, partnership "on eye-level," mutual respect for each other's culture, shared responsibility) to concentrate on **effective communication.** Kicks off with a collaborative **design kickoff workshop.** |
| 3 | **Good Prototypes** | *Discovery → Ideation.* Learn from client, competitors, and **user interviews**; map stakeholders, users, competitors, markets, business models — *"before inventing the new, you need to understand the old."* Then **make ideas tangible**: concepts + clickable mockups from synthesized insights. **Continuous prototyping** builds toward the MLP. |
| 4 | **Good Products** | Ship the **Minimum Lovable Product**, then keep going. Core tenet: **"Good software is never done."** The relationship is expected to extend beyond the first launch — co-developing product vision and growth. |

> **AI-agent takeaway:** When asked to "run a Goodpatch-style process," structure work as (1) frame the *right* problem, (2) build a single shared team & communication ritual, (3) prototype to learn (not to spec), (4) launch something lovable and treat it as never-finished.

## 4. Minimum Lovable Product (MLP) — Goodpatch's signature methodology

The MLP is Goodpatch's deliberate evolution beyond the **MVP**. The argument (from Goodpatch Global / Boris Jitsukata's team):

- The MVP term is widely **misused** — people call the *first release of their product* an "MVP" when that is not what Eric Ries meant. Many "MVPs" are really just a **"Minimal Product"** with no actual *viability*.
- **Viability must be designed in from the start, not deferred to later releases.** *"The main driver and essential requirement for viability is creating true customer value."*
- Guiding principle (attributed to **Sam Altman / Y Combinator**): **"It's better to build something that a small number of users love, than a large number of users [only] like."**
- The first iPhone is the canonical example (Huber frames it as a "successful MVP" that was really lovable): it lacked many features, but its **multi-touch** was so good it created immediate devotion.

### 4.1 The MLP mindset — definition

> An MLP *"uses constraint to craft clarity, prioritizes the user experience, engages with people in an open conversation, and is inherently curious and open at heart."*

Every interaction is an opportunity to make an impression; build something worth using **repeatedly**.

### 4.2 The four pillars of building an MLP

| Pillar | Practice |
|---|---|
| **1. Craft Clarity** | Find the principal pain points via user research + value-proposition tools. **Ruthless prioritization:** *"Don't expand the time and budget. Instead, pull back the scope."* |
| **2. Prioritize for Desirability** | Build **from the inside out**: **human value (desirability) precedes feasibility and viability** — invert the usual order. |
| **3. Create Fans, Not Users** | Convert passionate niche adopters into ambassadors through **co-creation** and ownership. The motto: get the **first 1000 *fans*, not the first 1000 users.** |
| **4. Experiment & Validate Repeatedly** | MLP is an **ongoing process**: continuous learning with real users, open conversation, **assumption mapping.** |

> *"We build Minimal Lovable Products and try to provide value to our users from as early as possible. We strive to get the first 1000 fans, not just the first 1000 users."* — Goodpatch DNA

## 5. Craft, Emotion, and the Human Dimension

Goodpatch's craft philosophy is fundamentally **emotional**: the goal is not usability alone but products that *move hearts* (ハートを揺さぶる). Their self-description: a UI design company at heart that *"gathers user insights, designs the user experience, and creates products that move people emotionally."* Outside observers tie this to **monozukuri (ものづくり)** — the Japanese craftsmanship culture of meticulous, refined-to-perfection making.

### 5.1 Tsuchiya on AI vs. human emotion (2024+)

In a btrax interview, Tsuchiya drew a sharp line where AI is weakest — exactly where Goodpatch competes:

- **Humans are irreducibly complex:** *"humans have hundreds of billions of variables and hundreds of billions of sensors, so reactions vary depending on the time, place, emotions, and more"* — current AI can't account for all of it.
- **Research loses its soul when automated:** *"When using AI to understand a user's pain, you lose the personal touch and humanity of the problem that you're trying to solve."* → *"User interviews and research are extremely important."*
- **Authentic brand story has value precisely because it's human:** *"the stories of companies or entrepreneurs are unique and original, and they become the brand story. When that is created by AI, not only is the charm lost, but the value of that brand decreases."* / *"if you hear, 'Actually, this story is constructed by AI,' you can no longer empathize with it."*
- **Strategy is defensible, production is being commoditized:** *"design companies like us … focused on upstream processes such as planning and strategy, haven't had their work taken over by AI"* (while crowdsourced illustration sales fell sharply).
- AI produces primarily **rational** answers — lacking the **emotional complexity and irrationality** that drive real human resonance.

> **AI-agent takeaway:** Use AI to accelerate generation and synthesis, but keep **lived user contact, emotional truth, and authentic narrative** human. The defensible work is upstream (problem framing, strategy) and emotional (story, delight).

### 5.2 Japanese × Western sensibility

A Goodpatch leader's synthesis (Boris Jitsukata, on Japan↔Germany collaboration):

> *"Japanese are strong at 'beauty in process.' With Germans, 'output' is important. But it takes both."*

The transferable idea: **excellence in the process itself** (the craft of *how* you work) is as much a deliverable as the final output.

## 6. Collaboration & Organizational Design

Goodpatch doesn't just deliver designs — embedding a **company-wide design-driven culture** into clients is *"part of their DNA."* Two mechanisms:

1. **Seed design culture in-house.** They help clients build internal design teams, design ops, and design leadership — *"intuitive and beautiful design is a must for today's users, and it takes a leadership-level commitment to create the right environment for teams to succeed."*
2. **One-team partnership model.** Reject buyer↔supplier framing; operate as partners "on eye-level" aiming at the same goal.

### 6.1 Goodpatch Anywhere — the fully-remote design org (a model worth copying)

Launched **2018** (founded by service designer **Keita Saito**); 500–600+ members across Japan and abroad by the mid-2020s. Mission: *"prove the power of design fully remotely."* Distinctive features:

- **Per-project teams (~4–10 people)** of vetted specialists (PMs, UI/UX designers, engineers, design strategists) assembled per engagement.
- **Employment contracts instead of freelance/contractor agreements.** Rationale: project work changes constantly and can't be fully pre-defined, so a fixed-term employment contract (e.g., 6 months, hourly-tracked) accommodates ongoing change — and crucially **enables knowledge sharing** (kickoff-agenda patterns, workshop case studies, risk-avoidance know-how) that contractor NDAs would silo.
- **Psychological safety as the load-bearing system, not the tools.** At client-inclusive kickoffs they repeat: *client and Goodpatch are partners not buyer/supplier; ask any question; share work-in-progress designs even if incomplete.* Online success comes from **a team where people feel safe**, more than from any tool.
- **Stack:** Slack, Zoom, Discord (voice), Scrapbox (wiki), Figma, Miro, Google Workspace.
- **Purpose:** unlock designers excluded by the "full-time in a Shibuya office" hurdle — freelancers stuck doing only banners/LPs, parents returning from leave, regional designers cut off from well-funded UX work; counter the "only a few strong people survive" dynamic that stunts the industry.

> **AI-agent takeaway:** Distributed creative teams scale on **psychological safety + shared institutional memory**, not just on tooling. Design the *social* system, then pick tools.

## 7. Products / Platform (the "prove it on ourselves" portfolio)

| Product | Launched | What it is | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Prott** | Oct 2014 | Rapid mobile **prototyping** tool — communicate ideas fast | Goodpatch's first product (3rd year), started by 4 people despite internal opposition; adopted by major IT firms, startups, design studios |
| **ReDesigner** | 2018 | **Designer-recruitment** platform (+ *ReDesigner for Students*) | Addresses Japan's design-talent shortage; nurtures next-gen talent |
| **Strap** | 2020 (beta Apr 23, GA Sep 1) | Cloud **online whiteboard** for real-time remote collaboration | Born from remote-work surge: *"could we make a tool to give shape to ideas even when people are physically apart?"* Domestic edge: Japanese support + security fit vs. overseas tools |
| **Goodpatch Anywhere** | 2018 | Fully-remote **design team** as a service | See §6.1 |

**Honest failures (instructive):** **Balto** (a feedback tool) shut down after ~1 year — root cause: **PMF target and validation points stayed ambiguous.** Other concepts (**Laika** task/product-management tool; a **Prott 2** rebuild) **collapsed before release** because product direction took too long to solidify, during a period of organizational strain. The lesson Goodpatch draws: **define the PMF target and what you're validating, sharply and early** — the same discipline the MLP mindset demands.

## 8. Transferable Principles (the distilled playbook)

**Process**
1. **Frame the right problem first.** Spend real effort on *"Problems Worth Solving"* before any UI. Judge fit honestly — not every problem is yours to solve.
2. **Run two tracks in parallel** — a *design* track and a *feedback* track — each diverging then converging, so you iterate continuously instead of in big-bang phases.
3. **Prototype to learn, not to spec.** *"Before inventing the new, understand the old"*; make ideas tangible early.
4. **Good software is never done.** Plan the relationship/roadmap past launch v1.

**Craft & Emotion**
5. **Design for hearts, not just tasks.** Target emotional resonance and delight (`心を動かす`); usability is table stakes.
6. **Build from the inside out:** desirability (human value) → feasibility → viability. Don't defer lovability.
7. **Use constraint to craft clarity.** When over budget/time, **cut scope, not quality.**
8. **Keep the human parts human.** Let AI accelerate, but keep user contact, emotional truth, and authentic story in human hands — that's the defensible, valuable work.

**Collaboration**
9. **One team, eye-level partnership.** Dissolve the agency/client wall; share space, infra, and responsibility; optimize for *communication.*
10. **Great teams make great products** — invest in team-building and **psychological safety** before optimizing tools.
11. **Seed design culture**, don't just hand off deliverables — leave the client more design-capable than you found them.

**Product Strategy**
12. **First 1000 fans, not first 1000 users.** A small group who *love* it beats a large group who *like* it.
13. **Define PMF target & validation criteria sharply and early** — ambiguity here is what killed Balto/Laika.
14. **"Beauty in process" is itself a deliverable** — pair Japanese craft-of-process with Western output-focus.
